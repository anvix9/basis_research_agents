# Research Brief: Scholasticide in Palestine 1922–2025

## Problem Statement

The research question demands historical evidence spanning 103 years (British Mandate 1922 through October 2025) demonstrating systematic targeting of Palestinian educational institutions—termed "scholasticide"—and classification as war crime under international law. The problem is presently unanswerable: the October 2025 endpoint references a non-existent peace agreement; the conceptual framework lacks academic foundation; archival evidence remains inaccessible across colonial and occupation periods; and international law has not operationalized protections for educational infrastructure as a distinct prosecutable category.

## Current State

**Established:**
- Geneva Protocol I Articles 52–53 protect civilian objects and cultural/educational infrastructure from attack
- Rome Statute Article 8(2)(b)(ix) prohibits intentionally attacking buildings dedicated to education
- ICC *Prosecutor v. Al Mahdi* (2016) established war crimes jurisprudence for intentional destruction of protected sites, with potential analogical application to universities
- Satellite imagery technology (Sentinel-2, Planet Labs, Maxar) exists at sufficient resolution (0.3–10m) for retrospective damage assessment in Gaza
- ICJ jurisprudence confirms convergence of international humanitarian law (IHL) and international human rights law (IHRL) during armed conflict

**Contested:**
- Gaza's legal status post-2005 disengagement: whether occupation law (Fourth Geneva Convention) or non-international armed conflict law (Protocol II) applies—determining which protection regime governs educational infrastructure
- Whether "scholasticide" requires distinct legal classification or whether existing civilian object protections suffice
- Applicability of Al Mahdi cultural property jurisprudence to educational infrastructure across different treaty bases (1954 Hague Convention vs. Geneva Protocol I)
- Whether systematic educational destruction constitutes crimes against humanity under Rome Statute Article 7 or requires autonomous legal category
- Universities' dual-use potential in asymmetric warfare (strategic targets vs. purely civilian objects)

**Unknown:**
- Whether archival documentation exists for British Mandate educational violence (1922–1948)
- Comprehensive damage documentation for Gaza's educational infrastructure across multiple conflicts (2008–2024)
- Whether forensic methodologies have been applied to establish systematic targeting patterns vs. collateral damage
- Existence and accessibility of Israeli military operational doctrine governing targeting protocols for educational facilities
- Whether any ICC preliminary examinations address educational infrastructure destruction in Palestine context
- Existence of the October 9, 2025 peace agreement (contradicted by all available sources)

## Key Gaps

**Gap 1: Conceptual Absence (Highest Priority)**
Complete lack of scholarly framework defining "scholasticide" as distinct violence category. Existing Geneva Conventions protections for civilian objects and cultural property may be adequate, but no scholarship tests their application to systematic educational destruction. The question assumes classification as war crime is possible but provides no framework for evaluating this assumption.

**Gap 2: Historical Silence**
Zero archival scholarship documents Palestinian educational institution development or targeting across 1922–2025. The corpus contains 17 history papers addressing only philosophy of science. No engagement with British Mandate administrative records, League of Nations documentation, Ottoman archives, or contemporary conflict documentation. The scholarly community studying this problem does not exist.

**Gap 3: Legal Doctrine Operationalization**
No scholarship applies ICTY/ICC cultural property jurisprudence to educational infrastructure. Al Mahdi precedent provides potential template, but analogical reasoning from cultural/religious property (1954 Hague Convention) to educational infrastructure (Geneva Protocol I) remains judicially untested. No analysis of whether systematic educational destruction meets Rome Statute Article 7 "widespread or systematic attack" thresholds as crimes against humanity.

**Gap 4: Methodological Infrastructure**
Absence of forensic damage assessment protocols, satellite imagery applications, and documentation frameworks meeting war crimes evidentiary standards for Gaza's educational infrastructure. Technologies exist (Sentinel-2, automated damage assessment algorithms); their deployment to establish systematic targeting, prove intent, and meet ICC thresholds remains undemonstrated.

**Gap 5: Area Studies Knowledge**
Complete disciplinary silence on Israel-Palestine conflict dynamics, occupation law, Hamas governance, Gaza's legal status, and 103-year regional history. Zero Arabic or Hebrew language sources. No engagement with Middle East studies expertise necessary for contextualizing educational institution development and violence patterns.

## Viable Approaches

**Approach 1: Temporal Boundary Correction (Feasible, Immediate)**
Verify whether October 9, 2025 peace agreement exists in UN documentation, governmental statements, or news archives. This straightforward fact-checking will likely confirm the endpoint is factual error, requiring reset to documented historical record (likely 2024 Gaza conflict as actual endpoint). Essential precondition for all subsequent research.

**Approach 2: Crimes Against Humanity Framework (Partially Feasible, High Priority)**
Rather than pursuing autonomous "scholasticide" classification, investigate whether systematic educational destruction qualifies as crimes against humanity under Rome Statute Article 7 by demonstrating: (a) widespread or systematic attack on civilian population, (b) intentional targeting of educational buildings as civilian infrastructure, (c) pattern establishing systematic nature. Requires no new legal doctrine—applies existing framework. Medium-term legal scholarship with doctrinal development.

**Approach 3: Legal Analogical Reasoning from Al Mahdi (Partially Feasible, Medium Risk)**
Develop systematic analogical extension from ICC's Al Mahdi cultural property destruction conviction to educational infrastructure protection. Plausible but judicially untested—addresses treaty base differences and challenges categorical extension. Success would establish universities as specifically protected legal category equivalent to hospitals or cultural property. Sophisticated legal work but higher doctrinal uncertainty.

**Approach 4: Satellite Forensics Methodology (Partially Feasible, Medium-Term Technical)**
Deploy digital humanities forensic methodology combining satellite imagery temporal analysis (Sentinel-2, Planet Labs, Maxar), automated damage assessment using deep learning, and geospatial analysis. Technical components proven in Syria/Yemen conflicts but require: (1) protocol development specific to Gaza educational infrastructure, (2) validation methodology, (3) frameworks producing legally admissible evidence for ICC standards. Medium-term technical research project operationalizing proven technologies.

**Approach 5: Reverse-Chronological Evidence Strategy (Feasible, Recommended)**
Abandon impossible 1922 starting point. Begin with satellite-documented recent conflicts (2023–24) and work backward only to periods where archival access can be demonstrated. Prioritize: (a) 2008–2024 damage documentation from UN OCHA, NGO reports, satellite imagery, (b) post-1967 occupation period educational restrictions (accessible from UN archives), (c) British Mandate only if archival access feasible. Yields defensible evidence base without temporal impossibility constraints.

## Recommended Trajectory

Research capacity for this problem requires simultaneous infrastructure development across four interdependent domains, not sequential research within existing frameworks. The immediate task is **foundational infrastructure building**, not evidence synthesis:

1. **Immediate (Months 1-3):** Correct temporal boundary through fact-checking; establish actual historical endpoint (likely 2024). Simultaneously conduct legal landscape mapping of Geneva Protocol I Article 52–53 applications and Rome Statute Article 7 crimes against humanity standards as baseline.

2. **Near-term (Months 3-9):** Develop crimes against humanity prosecution framework. Operationalize satellite forensics methodology using templates from Syria/Yemen documentation projects. Initiate Gaza damage documentation compilation from accessible sources (UN fact-finding missions, NGO reports, satellite archives).

3. **Medium-term (Months 9-18):** Conduct legal analogical reasoning work extending Al Mahdi precedent to educational infrastructure (if courts not barriers to theoretical development). Pilot methodologies on recent conflict documentation (2023-24). Establish archival access protocols for post-1967 occupation period.

4. **Long-term (18+ months):** Only if archival access secured, conduct historical research on post-1948 educational institution development and targeting patterns. Do not attempt British Mandate period without demonstrated access to Ottoman/British colonial archives.

**Critical uncertainty:** Whether sufficient primary source material exists in accessible archives—particularly Palestinian institutional records, Israeli military operational documentation, and British Mandate educational files—to construct evidentiary base meeting war crimes prosecution standards. Without this, the project remains conceptually and legally viable but empirically hollow.

## Key Uncertainties

**Temporal Contradiction:** October 9, 2025 peace agreement has no documented existence. Uncertain whether this is speculative endpoint, factual error, or refers to undocumented agreement—fundamentally undermines research scope definition.

**Legal Category Dilemma:** Unclear whether "scholasticide" requires distinct classification or whether existing civilian object protections under Geneva Conventions are adequate but unapplied. If distinct classification required, must establish either: (a) inadequacy of Geneva protections (undemonstrated), or (b) Rome Statute Article 7 crimes against humanity qualification (requires forensic evidence of systematic pattern lacking in corpus). Both paths blocked without foundational work.

**Retroactivity Impossibility:** 103-year timespan demands applying 1949 and 1998 legal standards retroactively to pre-codification periods (1922-1948), violating nullum crimen sine lege principle. Requires either abandoning pre-codification evidence or developing novel retroactive legal framework contradicting international criminal law fundamentals.

**Archive Accessibility:** Unknown whether British Mandate educational records, Palestinian institutional archives, and Israeli military operational doctrine are accessible. This single uncertainty determines whether research is feasible—without primary source access, only speculative analysis is possible.

**Epistemological Exclusion:** Corpus excludes all Arabic and Hebrew sources, creating structurally incomplete evidence base excluding participant perspectives. Any conclusions necessarily biased toward external observer viewpoints rather than institutional and perpetrator voices.

**Evidentiary Standards:** War crimes prosecution requires proving systematic pattern, intent, and non-military-necessity. No protocols established for meeting ICC standards in educational infrastructure context. Unclear whether satellite damage assessment, NGO witness testimony, and UN fact-finding reports meet prosecutorial thresholds.

**Dual-Use Problem:** Unresolved tension between universities as purely civilian objects (protected under Article 52) and as potential strategic targets in asymmetric warfare (intelligence, resistance organization, tunnel access). No framework distinguishes legitimate military targeting from scholasticide claims.
