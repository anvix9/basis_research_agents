# Research Brief: Scholasticide in Gaza (1922-2025)

## Problem Statement

The research question seeks historical evidence supporting prosecution of educational destruction and "massacre of university students" in Gaza as war crimes under international law across a 103-year span (1922-2025). However, investigation reveals the question embeds unexamined assumptions: "scholasticide" lacks recognized legal definition; "massacre" has no threshold distinct from genocide/crimes against humanity; university students are not a protected demographic category in international humanitarian law; and the conflict's legal classification (international armed conflict vs. counter-terrorism operation) remains fundamentally contested, determining which protections apply.

## Current State

**Known**: International legal framework protects educational institutions through Hague Convention IV (1907), Geneva Convention IV (1949), Additional Protocol I Articles 52-53 (1977), and Rome Statute Articles 8(2)(b)(ii) and (ix). Genocide jurisprudence (Krstić 2001, Bosnia v. Serbia 2007) establishes intent-inference from systematic patterns. ICC has jurisdictional basis for Gaza through Palestine's Rome Statute accession (2015). Post-October 2023 escalation generated scholasticide claims in critical scholarship (Shlaim 2025, Domínguez 2025, Hajir et al. 2025). Forensic protocols exist for mass grave investigation.

**Contested**: The legal classification of the Israeli-Palestinian conflict—international armed conflict, non-international armed conflict, or counter-terrorism operation—determines which IHL protections trigger and remains unresolved. Whether systematic educational destruction meets genocide thresholds (specific intent to destroy Palestinians as group) versus crimes against humanity versus "merely" war crimes divides scholars. Whether Protocol I Article 1(4) applies (classifying Palestinian resistance as anti-colonial struggle) is disputed; Israel has not ratified Protocol I. Whether Hamas designation affects protected status of "Hamas-affiliated" educational institutions remains unresolved.

**Unknown**: No longitudinal database documents 1922-2025 educational destruction patterns necessary for intent-inference. British Mandate educational policies (1922-1948) have not been analyzed for systematic restriction patterns. Forensic standards for identifying university students as distinct victim demographic do not exist. The October 9, 2025 peace agreement text is unavailable or non-existent; implications for retrospective accountability cannot be assessed. Actual scale of university student deaths using forensic standards remains undocumented. Whether proportionality/military necessity defenses would succeed for systematic educational targeting lacks ICC precedent.

## Key Gaps (Ranked by Significance)

1. **Conflict classification bottleneck**: International vs. non-international armed conflict vs. counter-terrorism operation determination is foundational—all downstream legal protections depend on it. Unresolved despite decades of debate, this creates legal limbo where educational destruction's status cannot be determined.

2. **Forensic methodology absence**: No protocol identifies university students as distinct victim category. Intent-inference frameworks from genocide law (Krstić, Bosnia v. Serbia) require systematic pattern documentation; without methodology for student victim identification, "massacre of university students" claims cannot meet evidentiary thresholds.

3. **Definitional incoherence**: "Scholasticide" and "massacre" lack legal definitions. Must be prosecuted under genocide/crimes against humanity/war crimes frameworks that may not capture educational destruction's systematic character. No treaty amendment process has begun; concept remains at theoretical advocacy stage.

4. **Temporal documentation gap**: No comprehensive longitudinal database traces educational destruction across 1922-2025. Partial sources exist (British Mandate archives, UNRWA records 1950-present), but 1948-1967 documentation is fragmented, Israeli military targeting data is classified. Gap prevents establishing whether patterns are systematic versus episodic.

5. **Settler-colonial framework inoperability**: Critical scholarship (Sarkin 2008, Tuck & Yang 2012, Shlaim 2025) identifies settler-colonial analysis as essential, but Rome Statute/Geneva Conventions do not recognize "settler-colonialism" as legal category. Creates theory-law translation gap; Mandate legal architecture remains incommensurable with contemporary accountability frameworks.

## Viable Approaches (Passed Feasibility Evaluation)

**Partially Feasible—Highest Priority**:

- **Cumulative war crimes prosecution** (Rome Statute Articles 8(2)(b)(ii), 8(2)(b)(ix)): Legal framework exists, ICC has jurisdiction. Barriers: Israel's non-cooperation as non-State Party, unresolved conflict classification affecting applicable Articles, no ICC jurisprudence on cumulative educational destruction charges, untested proportionality/military necessity defenses. This is the most viable legal pathway despite obstacles.

- **Protocol I Article 1(4) legal argumentation**: Develop comprehensive framework demonstrating counter-terrorism classification violates Protocol I (which explicitly recognizes anti-colonial/anti-occupation resistance as international armed conflict). Scholarly analysis supports this; textual basis exists. Barriers: Israel has not ratified Protocol I (lacks binding force), no tribunal has ruled on Gaza's Article 1(4) status, classification remains highly politicized.

- **Forensic-documentary protocol development**: Create standards for identifying university students as distinct demographic cohort within casualty datasets using anthropological categorization methods and general forensics protocols. Barriers: Requires identity verification in destroyed contexts, enrollment records may be inaccessible, age-overlap challenges, no legal precedent certifies students as protected category.

- **Educational infrastructure longitudinal database** (1922-2025): Assemble partial archives from British Mandate records (UK National Archives), UNRWA operational records (1950-present), Palestinian Authority documentation. Barriers: 1948-1967 records fragmented, Israeli military data classified/inaccessible, no unified categorization system across incompatible sources. Addresses pattern documentation gap but with incomplete coverage.

**Fully Feasible—Secondary Priority**:

- **Human capital destruction economic analysis**: Quantify scholasticide's impact on Palestinian human capital formation, innovation capacity, intergenerational wealth. Established economic methodologies apply; addresses reparations quantification. Limitations: Attribution challenges (separating scholasticide from broader blockade impacts), counterfactual construction difficulties.

**Unfeasible**:

- **Genocide prosecution via "conditions of life"**: Tribunals narrowly interpret Genocide Convention Article II(c) to require immediate survival threats (starvation, medical deprivation), never extended to long-term educational deprivation. Scholasticide would require specific group-destruction intent with longitudinal pattern evidence that does not exist. Unfeasible under current jurisprudence.

## Recommended Trajectory

**Phase 1—Immediate (Resolve Foundational Bottleneck)**:
Pursue authoritative tribunal ruling or scholarly consensus on conflict classification. Until international armed conflict vs. counter-terrorism operation is determined, educational destruction's legal status remains indeterminate. This is the critical prerequisite—all subsequent legal strategy depends on it.

**Phase 2—Concurrent (Build Evidentiary Foundation)**:
Commission forensic-documentary protocol for educational targeting pattern analysis. Accept that complete 1922-2025 documentation is impossible (1948-1967 fragmented, Israeli data classified) but establish forensic standards for what can be proven. Distinguish intentional targeting from collateral damage through cumulative pattern analysis even without student-specific victim identification. Leverage remote sensing for infrastructure destruction, diaspora witness testimony, Palestinian Authority/UNRWA document preservation.

**Phase 3—Prosecutorial Strategy (Shift to Existing Categories)**:
Abandon "scholasticide as distinct crime" framing. Operationalize cumulative war crimes charges under Articles 8(2)(b)(ii) and (ix) using established doctrine. This pathway is viable now versus theoretically waiting for treaty amendments. Develop "joint criminal enterprise" prosecution strategy similar to ICTY model, aggregating multiple school/university attacks across time/space as unified pattern exceeding any plausible military necessity.

**Phase 4—Subsidiary (Address Political-Legal Contingencies)**:
Analyze October 9, 2025 peace agreement (if extant) for amnesty provisions, transitional justice mechanisms, complementarity implications. Investigate whether peace-conditionality frameworks (Serbia-Montenegro EU accession conditioned on ICTY cooperation) could be operationalized. Develop alternative accountability mechanisms beyond ICC if retrospective prosecution is foreclosed—truth commissions, reparations frameworks, educational restoration requirements as conflict resolution conditions.

## Key Uncertainties

1. **ICC cooperation barriers vs. jurisdictional basis**: Jurisdictional authority exists through Palestine's accession, but Israel's non-cooperation creates evidentiary insurmountability. Whether open-source intelligence, diaspora testimony, and third-party document preservation can overcome this remains untested.

2. **Conflict classification resolution likelihood**: This foundational bottleneck may be permanently unresolved due to political stakes. If so, educational destruction remains in legal limbo regardless of evidence—making strategic shift toward non-criminal accountability mechanisms necessary.

3. **Genocide claims viability**: Restrictive "conditions of life" jurisprudence and absence of longitudinal intent documentation create compounding barriers making genocide prosecution unfeasible. War crimes pathway is more realistic but significantly narrower in scope/gravity.

4. **Peace agreement implications opacity**: October 9, 2025 agreement text's availability, content, and legal implications are completely unknown. Could contain amnesty provisions precluding prosecution. Affects entire retrospective accountability calculus.

5. **Proportionality defense applicability**: No ICC jurisprudence exists on cumulative educational destruction charges or whether systematic destruction exceeds military necessity defenses. Defense success rates are unpredictable.

6. **Settler-colonial framework legal operationalization**: Conceptually essential to understanding Mandate origins, but cannot be prosecuted within Rome Statute structures. Creates perpetual theory-law gap—most compelling analytical frameworks remain outside formal legal remedy structures.
