Critically, the temporal gap (GS-6) suggests intellectual lineages may have been lost between early systems thinking and contemporary agricultural research, preventing cumulative knowledge-building. The overall pattern suggests not gradual knowledge accumulation but paradigmatic separation: complexity theory and agricultural research operate as distinct knowledge communities with minimal theoretical exchange, creating a situation where powerful analytical tools remain unused and urgent sustainability questions remain poorly conceptualized.
