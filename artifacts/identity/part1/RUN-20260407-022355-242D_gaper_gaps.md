### What are the fundamental constitutive elements of intelligence according to established psychometric models?
**Status:** answered
Well-established through Spearman's g (1904) and hierarchical Gf-Gc models (McGrew & Flanagan 1998). General intelligence (g) identified as common factor across cognitive tasks; hierarchical models distinguish fluid intelligence (Gf: reasoning, novel problem-solving) from crystallized intelligence (Gc: knowledge, learned skills). Methodology is sound, widely replicated, and forms basis of modern psychometrics. However, these models are descriptive-structural, not explanatory. They identify what intelligence is (factorial structure) but not why variation exists or what underlying mechanisms produce it. Identity has never been proposed as a candidate mechanism.

This represents a disciplinary split: philosophy of personal identity treats consciousness as identity-constitutive, but philosophy of mind/cognitive neuroscience treats consciousness as independent phenomenon. Partial answer: Lockean tradition says yes, modern consciousness studies say question is orthogonal. No resolution or integration attempted.
