### ⚠ Partially Feasible

- **Proposal:** PROP-1B1C1BAF: The Neural Dissociation Hypothesis posits that identity maintenance and intelligent cognition...
  **Verdict reason:** Default mode network (DMN) association with self-referential processing is well-established (Buckner et al. 2008, Andrews-Hanna et al. 2010). Executive control networks linked to intelligence are documented (Duncan 2010, Jung & Haier 2007). However, the critical prediction—that these networks are 'anatomically and functionally dissociable'—is contradicted by evidence of DMN-ECN interactions and anti-correlations (Fox et al. 2005). The networks are not independent but dynamically coupled. Furthermore, no studies directly correlate identity disruption (measured independently) with intelligence performance while controlling for network activity.
  **Weakest link:** The assumption of functional dissociability contradicts documented DMN-ECN coupling. No studies demonstrate that selectively disrupting identity-related DMN activity leaves intelligence-related ECN activity intact, or vice versa.
  **To change verdict:** Lesion studies or TMS showing selective disruption of identity without intelligence effects (or vice versa); double dissociation in neuropsychological populations; neuroimaging studies measuring both identity coherence and intelligence performance showing orthogonal neural substrates; computational models demonstrating independence.

- **Proposal:** PROP-D9A5B5F0: Social Identity Scaling Hypothesis: Social identity effects on cognition demonstrated in...
  **Verdict reason:** Social identity effects on specific cognitive tasks are well-demonstrated (Steele & Aronson 1995 stereotype threat; Tajfel & Turner 1979 categorization effects). However, these effects are domain-specific performance decrements, not demonstrations of altered general intelligence or psychometric g. Stereotype threat studies typically show performance decrements of 0.3-0.5 SD on specific tests, not global intelligence changes. The 'scaling' claim—that these effects represent 'domain-specific expressions of identity-intelligence coupling'—extrapolates beyond demonstrated evidence. No studies show social identity manipulations affecting g-loaded tasks across multiple domains or altering g-factor structure.
  **Weakest link:** The claim that domain-specific performance effects scale to general intelligence lacks evidence. Stereotype threat affects test performance but has not been shown to affect underlying g or intelligence structure.
  **To change verdict:** Studies showing social identity manipulations affect g-factor estimates across comprehensive test batteries; factor analytic studies showing identity manipulations alter g-structure; longitudinal studies showing identity changes correlate with g-changes; neuroimaging showing identity manipulations affect neural correlates of g (not just domain-specific performance).

- **Proposal:** PROP-B919E1A2: Identity-continuity over time is achieved through functional information integration...

Autobiographical memory's role in self-continuity is documented (Conway & Pleydell-Pearce 2000). The mechanisms are real but their relationship to identity (as opposed to general autobiographical memory) is not established.
  **Weakest link:** No studies demonstrate that individuals with impaired memory consolidation or temporal binding specifically lose identity-continuity (rather than just memory). No correlations between process efficiency and identity measures exist.
  **To change verdict:** Studies of amnesia patients showing identity disruption specifically (not just memory loss); neuropsychological dissociations showing memory intact but identity disrupted, or vice versa; correlations between memory consolidation efficiency and validated identity coherence measures; experimental manipulations of temporal binding affecting identity but not general memory.

- **Proposal:** PROP-36462B60: Mind-brain identity revival via neuroscience: Empirically test type-identity theory...
  **Verdict reason:** Type-identity theory (Place 1956, Smart 1959) faced multiple empirical and conceptual problems leading to its abandonment: multiple realizability (Putnam 1967 - same mental state can be realized in different physical states), species variation in neural substrates for similar psychological states, and individual variation in neural implementation. Modern high-resolution neuroimaging confirms these problems: neural correlates of psychological states show substantial individual variation, context-dependence, and distributed implementation. The proposal suggests 'determining whether specific psychological identity types map to specific brain state types' but this contradicts 70 years of neuroscience showing that psychological states are multiply realizable, context-dependent, and individually variable.
  **Weakest link:** The core prediction of type-identity (identical psychological state types → identical brain state types) is contradicted by massive evidence of individual variation, multiple realizability, and neural degeneracy in implementing psychological functions.
  **To change verdict:** Discovery of invariant neural signatures across all individuals for specific psychological states (contradicting current neuroscience); resolution of multiple realizability problem; demonstration that individual neural variation is measurement noise rather than genuine implementation differences. Essentially, would require overturning current neuroscience consensus.

- **Proposal:** PROP-E7453B04: Operationalize standpoint epistemology's 'critical consciousness' construct through...
  **Verdict reason:** Standpoint epistemology (Collins 1990, Freire 1973) is a theoretical/philosophical framework about epistemic positioning, not a cognitive psychological framework with operationalizable constructs. 'Critical consciousness' is not defined as a measurable cognitive capacity in the standpoint literature. The proposal attempts to translate philosophical/sociological concepts into cognitive assessment batteries, but the source theories don't provide operational definitions, don't specify cognitive mechanisms, and don't make predictions about individual differences in cognitive performance. Creating a 'critical consciousness cognitive assessment battery' would be inventing constructs not present in the source theory, then claiming to test the theory - this is circular. Furthermore, no evidence suggests marginalized identity positions show enhanced performance on any cognitive measures.
  **Weakest link:** Complete mismatch between standpoint epistemology (social epistemology, not cognitive psychology) and cognitive assessment (individual differences in cognitive capacities). The proposal conflates epistemic positioning with cognitive ability without theoretical or empirical basis.
  **To change verdict:** Standpoint theorists themselves would need to operationalize critical consciousness as cognitive capacities (not epistemic positions); pilot studies showing marginalized groups outperform dominant groups on proposed measures; theoretical development linking social epistemology to cognitive psychology; convergent validity evidence that proposed measures assess something distinct from existing cognitive constructs.
