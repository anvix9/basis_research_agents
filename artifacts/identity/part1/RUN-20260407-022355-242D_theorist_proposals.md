## Overview

The pipeline reveals a profound 120-year disciplinary silence: intelligence research and identity research have developed in complete isolation despite both addressing core aspects of mind. This separation creates extraordinary theoretical opportunity. The proposals below exploit three strategic entry points. First, **mechanistic bridging proposals** leverage modern neuroscience and computational methods to empirically test identity-intelligence coupling through neural substrates, clinical populations, and developmental trajectories—making theoretically plausible relationships empirically tractable. Second, **paradigm collision proposals** directly confront contradictions between stable trait models (psychometric intelligence) and context-dependent construction models (social/narrative identity), forcing resolution through standpoint epistemology validation or identity-modulated intelligence frameworks. Third, **foundational integration proposals** revisit abandoned philosophical frameworks (mind-brain identity theory, Lockean memory-consciousness criterion) with modern empirical tools, potentially unifying ontological and psychological identity theories while grounding intelligence in identity-maintenance architecture. These proposals are not incremental—they target the explanatory gap at intelligence research's core: we can describe factorial structure (what intelligence is) but cannot explain variation origins (why individuals differ). Identity remains a completely unexplored candidate mechanism.
