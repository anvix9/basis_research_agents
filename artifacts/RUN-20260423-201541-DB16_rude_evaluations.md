Every human tissue-based proposal faces severe feasibility barriers due to tissue availability—the complete absence of published work on connexin 43 in these syndromes suggests either access barriers have prevented investigation or preliminary attempts found no phenotype. The TGF-β hypothesis (PROP-D8458504) requires basic demonstration that TGF-β regulates connexin 43 before pursuing disease-specific studies. Current evidence: essentially zero. The field has not investigated whether the problem actually exists.

  **Verdict reason:** This proposal requires simultaneous multi-modal assessment combining: optical mapping (requiring intact, viable tissue with voltage-sensitive dyes), high-resolution cardiac MRI, dye coupling assays, and molecular analysis on the same samples. Optical mapping of human cardiac or aortic tissue requires fresh, viable tissue maintained under physiological conditions—this is extremely challenging with human samples and typically only achievable with surgical specimens processed within minutes of excision.   **To change verdict:** Published methodology demonstrating successful integration of even two of these techniques (e.g., optical mapping + molecular analysis, or dye coupling + structural imaging) on the same human cardiac or aortic tissue samples, with validated protocols for tissue handling, preservation, and sequential or parallel measurements
