## Phases

### Archaic Pre-Philosophical Intelligence (~750-500 BCE)
Intelligence understood as practical cunning (metis), situational resourcefulness, and embodied skill in Homeric tradition. No abstract categories yet; intelligence inseparable from action and context.
*Transition driver: Emergence of philosophical logos requiring abstract conceptualization of intelligence separate from particular contexts*

### Pre-Socratic Cosmological Intelligence (~500-400 BCE)
Intelligence elevated from human capacity to cosmic principle. Heraclitus establishes logos as universal rational order; Anaxagoras makes nous transcendent organizing force; Empedocles and Democritus offer materialist alternatives grounding intelligence in nature itself (sympathy and atomic configuration).
*Transition driver: Socratic-Platonic turn inward to human soul and epistemological questions, creating need for hierarchical ordering of cognitive capacities*

### Platonic-Aristotelian Canonical Taxonomy (~400-300 BCE)
Establishment of foundational categorical distinctions that dominated Western thought for two millennia: theoretical vs practical intelligence (episteme/sophia vs phronesis); hierarchical soul-types (vegetative-sensitive-rational); intelligence positioned within nature through physis as immanent principle; divine intelligence as pure self-reflexive thought establishing absolute standard. Three-tiered hierarchy: divine/cosmic/human intelligence.
*Transition driver: Collapse of classical polis and rise of Hellenistic kingdoms requiring new frameworks for individual intellectual perfection*

### Hellenistic Ethical Intelligence (~300 BCE-200 CE)
Shift from cosmic/metaphysical to therapeutic/practical conception. Intelligence as tool for achieving ataraxia (Epicureans) or living according to logos (Stoics). Medical tradition (Hippocratic/Galenic) develops parallel empirical account of intelligence as embodied physiological process tied to humoral balance.
*Transition driver: Neoplatonic revival reintroducing transcendent metaphysics and emanationist hierarchy*

### Neoplatonic Emanationist Synthesis (~200-500 CE)
Plotinus establishes hierarchical emanation from The One through Intelligence (Nous) to Soul. Intelligence becomes cosmic hypostasis; human intelligence participates through ascent. Creates framework for positioning human intelligence between material nature and transcendent divine. Alexander of Aphrodisias makes agent intellect divine and external, creating problem of access.
*Transition driver: Christianization of Roman Empire requiring reconciliation of Greek philosophy with monotheistic theology*

### Patristic Christian Transformation (~400-1000 CE)
Augustine establishes divine illumination as alternative to abstraction; human intelligence dependent on God's light for grasping eternal truths. Intelligence no longer autonomous capacity but participatory relationship with divine source. Western tradition loses Greek texts; intelligence theory freezes in Augustinian framework.
*Transition driver: Translation movement bringing Arabic-preserved Aristotle to Latin West via Islamic and Jewish intermediaries*

### Islamic-Jewish Medieval Synthesis (~800-1200 CE)
Avicenna synthesizes Aristotle with Neoplatonism creating emanationist hierarchy from divine intelligence through celestial intellects to Active Intellect governing sublunary realm. Maimonides adapts for Judaism. Human intelligence achieves perfection through conjunction with separate Active Intellect. Debate over whether Active Intellect is individual or cosmic monopsychism (Averroes).
*Transition driver: Recovery of full Aristotelian corpus in Latin West creating crisis requiring Christian theological reconciliation*

### Scholastic Christian-Aristotelian Synthesis (~1200-1500 CE)
Aquinas establishes definitive synthesis: individual human soul possesses own rational intellect (against Averroist monopsychism) while maintaining participation in divine illumination; agent intellect individualized within each person; intelligence grounded in embodied soul; physis as God-created natural order positioning human intelligence between animals and angels in Great Chain of Being.
*Transition driver: Renaissance humanism and Protestant Reformation fragmenting scholastic consensus; revival of classical texts bypassing medieval interpretations*

### Early Modern Mechanistic Turn (~1500-1700 CE)
Descartes separates thinking substance (res cogitans) from extended substance (res extensa); intelligence becomes defining essence of human separated from mechanistic nature. Hobbes and materialists attempt to reduce intelligence to matter in motion. Nature reconceived as machine-like mechanism rather than organism with immanent physis. Human intelligence problematically positioned: either supernatural exception to mechanical nature or reducible to it.
*Transition driver: Empiricist epistemology demanding account of how intelligence arises from sensory experience; evolutionary geology/biology challenging fixed essences*

### Enlightenment Faculty Psychology (~1700-1850 CE)
Intelligence fragmented into faculties (memory, imagination, judgment, reason). Kant establishes transcendental conditions for possible experience; intelligence structures experience rather than passively receiving it. Romantic reaction (Herder, Schelling) reintroduces organic conception of nature with intelligence immanent rather than transcendent. Phrenology attempts empirical mapping of mental faculties to brain regions.
*Transition driver: Darwinian evolution requiring naturalistic account of intelligence continuous across species; experimental psychology establishing laboratory methods*

### Evolutionary Naturalization (~1850-1900 CE)
Darwin positions human intelligence within nature as evolved capacity continuous with animals. Spencer applies evolutionary framework to mental development. Galton initiates psychometrics and differential psychology. Intelligence transitions from philosophical essence to measurable natural phenomenon varying quantitatively. Phylogenetic continuity replaces categorical human-animal divide.
*Transition driver: Psychometric movement and IQ testing establishing quantitative individual differences; behaviorist rejection of introspection*

### Psychometric Operationalization (~1900-1950 CE)
Binet develops intelligence testing; Spearman establishes g-factor theory. Intelligence operationalized as measurable trait predicting academic/occupational performance. Fierce nature-nurture debates. Behaviorists (Watson, Skinner) attempt to eliminate intelligence as explanatory concept, reducing to learned associations. Gestalt psychology preserves holistic cognitive processes against atomistic reduction.
*Transition driver: Cognitive revolution establishing mind as information processor; computer metaphor replacing behaviorist black box*

### Cognitive Information Processing (~1950-1980 CE)
Mind reconceived as computational information processor. Turing establishes functional equivalence criterion; AI attempts to replicate intelligence mechanically. Multiple intelligences theories (Guilford, Gardner) fragment unitary g. Piaget maps developmental stages. Chomsky argues for innate language capacity challenging blank slate empiricism. Intelligence naturalized as neural computation but categories multiply.
*Transition driver: Neuroscience imaging technologies enabling direct observation of brain activity; evolutionary psychology applying adaptationist framework to cognition*

### Neuroscientific Localization (~1980-2010 CE)
fMRI and neuroimaging map cognitive functions to brain regions. Embodied/enactive cognition challenges computational metaphor, reintroducing body and environment. Emotional intelligence (Goleman) and social intelligence categories emerge. Evolutionary psychology explains intelligence as domain-specific adaptations rather than general capacity. Animal cognition research demonstrates sophisticated intelligence across species, blurring human exceptionalism.
*Transition driver: AI renaissance with deep learning achieving superhuman performance on specific tasks; comparative cognition revealing convergent evolution of intelligence; philosophical attention to machine consciousness*

### Multiple Intelligence Ontologies (~2010-present)
Fragmentation into competing frameworks: general intelligence (g) vs multiple intelligences; biological intelligence vs artificial intelligence; individual vs collective intelligence; natural vs artificial general intelligence (AGI). No consensus on fundamental nature. Human intelligence positioned as one variant among multiple possible implementations (biological, silicon, hybrid). Renewed attention to consciousness as potentially distinct from intelligence. Ancient questions resurface: Is intelligence unified or fragmented? Is human intelligence special? What is relationship between intelligence and nature?
*Transition driver: Ongoing - AI capabilities approaching and exceeding human performance forcing reconceptualization*

## Dead Ends

- **Democritean atomist reduction of intelligence to atomic configuration** (~400 BCE)
  Reason: Lacked empirical access to atoms; could not explain qualitative mental phenomena from quantitative atomic properties; no mechanism for intentionality or meaning
  Lesson: Pure materialism faces hard problem of consciousness and intentionality; reduction requires bridging principles not available to ancient atomism
- **Phrenological localization of mental faculties** (~1800-1850)
  Reason: Assumed direct correlation between skull shape and brain structure; faculties not discretely localized as hypothesized; lacked actual brain access
  Lesson: Correct intuition (brain localization) with wrong methodology; correlation without mechanism is insufficient; direct observation required
- **Behaviorist elimination of intelligence as explanatory concept** (~1920-1960)
  Reason: Could not account for language acquisition, creative problem-solving, or internal representations; stimulus-response paradigm too impoverished
  Lesson: Methodological parsimony taken to eliminativist extreme; internal states cannot be wished away by methodological fiat
- **Classical AI symbolic logic approach to general intelligence** (~1960-1990)
  Reason: Knowledge acquisition bottleneck; brittleness; frame problem; combinatorial explosion; lacked flexibility and generalization
  Lesson: Intelligence requires more than formal logic; embodiment, learning, and sub-symbolic processing essential; rules insufficient
- **Unitary g-factor as complete account of intelligence** (~1900-present (contested))
  Reason: Multiple intelligences research shows dissociations; cultural bias in measurement; conflates statistical correlation with causal explanation
  Lesson: Statistical regularity does not prove single underlying essence; measurement artifacts can create false unity; context matters

## Recurring Patterns

- **Transcendent vs immanent intelligence: Is intelligence external ordering principle or internal capacity of nature?**
  Appearances: Pre-Socratic (Anaxagoras vs Empedocles), Medieval (separate Active Intellect vs individualized intellect), Modern (dualism vs materialism), Contemporary (strong AI vs embodied cognition)
  Why: Fundamental metaphysical question about relationship between mind and world; explanatory power vs ontological parsimony tension
- **Unitary vs multiple intelligence: Is intelligence single essence or heterogeneous capacities?**
  Appearances: Aristotle (unified nous vs multiple virtues), Enlightenment (unified reason vs faculties), 20th century (g-factor vs multiple intelligences), Contemporary (general AI vs domain-specific modules)
  Why: Tension between phenomenological diversity and theoretical parsimony; measurement practices shape ontological commitments
- **Theoretical vs practical intelligence: Is abstract contemplation superior to applied problem-solving?**
  Appearances: Plato/Aristotle (sophia vs phronesis), Medieval (contemplative vs active life), Modern (pure vs applied science), Contemporary (academic vs emotional/social intelligence)
  Why: Reflects social hierarchies valuing different forms of knowledge; who defines 'intelligence' stakes power claims
- **Human exceptionalism vs continuity with nature: Is human intelligence categorically unique or quantitatively superior?**
  Appearances: Ancient (rational soul vs animal sensation), Medieval (imago dei vs beasts), Cartesian (res cogitans vs automata), Contemporary (consciousness vs computation)
  Why: Theological, ethical, and existential stakes in human uniqueness conflict with scientific naturalism
- **Intelligence as static essence vs developmental process: Is intelligence fixed capacity or emergent achievement?**
  Appearances: Ancient (eternal forms vs potentiality-actuality), Enlightenment (innate ideas vs empiricism), 20th century (nature vs nurture), Contemporary (genetics vs neuroplasticity)
  Why: Ontological assumptions about change and identity; political implications for education and social equality
- **Measurement problem: Can intelligence be quantified or does measurement distort its nature?**
  Appearances: Phrenology, IQ testing, cognitive neuroscience, AI benchmarks
  Why: Operationalization makes phenomena tractable but risks reifying artifacts; what we measure shapes what we think exists

## Methods Evolution

Intelligence research evolved from purely philosophical speculation to empirical investigation through several methodological revolutions. Ancient Greeks relied on conceptual analysis, introspection, and dialectical reasoning without systematic empirical testing. Hippocratic medical tradition introduced observational naturalism studying physiological correlates. Medieval scholastics added systematic commentary and logical analysis of authoritative texts. Early moderns introduced experimental philosophy and mechanistic explanation. 19th century brought comparative anatomy, evolutionary biology, and psychophysics creating quantitative methods. 20th century psychometrics operationalized intelligence as measurable individual differences through standardized testing. Cognitive revolution introduced experimental manipulation of information processing. Neuroscience added neuroimaging enabling direct observation of brain activity during cognitive tasks. Comparative cognition brought systematic cross-species behavioral experiments. Contemporary methods combine computational modeling, large-scale neuroimaging, machine learning, and philosophical analysis. Trend is toward methodological pluralism recognizing no single method captures full phenomenon. Critical tension: precision of measurement vs richness of phenomenon; replicability vs ecological validity; quantification vs qualitative understanding.
