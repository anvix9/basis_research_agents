## Challenged Assumptions

- **Assumption:** Electrical coupling alterations, if they exist, represent a continuous spectrum of disruption that scales with structural pathology severity
  **Challenge:** What if electrical communication shows threshold effects where moderate matrix defects are fully compensated but severe defects cause catastrophic electrical uncoupling?
  **If wrong:** Therapeutic interventions would need to maintain matrix integrity above threshold rather than incrementally improving it; patient risk stratification would be nonlinear; mild genotypes and severe genotypes might show binary electrical outcomes rather than gradation

- **Assumption:** Cardiac and aortic connexin alterations in these syndromes would show parallel changes because both tissues experience matrix defects from the same genetic cause
  **Challenge:** What if cardiac and aortic tissues show opposite connexin regulation—one showing increased coupling and one showing decreased coupling—due to different compensatory mechanisms?
  **If wrong:** Tissue-specific therapeutic approaches would be required; systemic gap junction modulation could improve one tissue while worsening another; cardiovascular manifestations might reflect opposing electrical adaptations creating system-level mismatch
