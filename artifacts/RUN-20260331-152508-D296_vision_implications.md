## Overview

The logical landscape reveals five critical fault lines: (1) **Definitional Collapse** — foundational concepts (intelligence, consciousness) operate under contradictory assumptions never reconciled (Spearman vs Gardner, Searle vs Dennett, individualist vs critical consciousness), meaning evaluations rest on incoherent foundations; (2) **Cultural Parochialism** — all frameworks are Western individualist computational, with non-Western, Indigenous, and collective models systematically excluded, rendering universality claims empirically untested; (3) **Empirical Vacuum** — philosophical positions (hard problem, intentionality, embodiment) have zero empirical adjudication criteria, forcing practical AI deployment to proceed without philosophical resolution or adopt functional stance by default; (4) **Dead End Vindication** — mechanistic approaches dismissed historically (Thorndike) are vindicated by deep learning, demanding re-examination of philosophical dismissals but none has occurred; (5) **Normative Absence** — descriptive AI capabilities advance without normative framework for human meaning/flourishing, optimizing means without evaluating ends. The strongest implications reveal not knowledge gaps but **logical contradictions and untested assumptions at the foundations** — we don't know what intelligence is (unitary vs multiple), whether consciousness exists as we conceive it (hard problem assumption untested), or if our evaluation criteria are universal vs anthropocentric. Second-order implications surface deeper instabilities: if consciousness is dissociable processes rather than unified phenomenon, binary conscious/not-conscious framings collapse; if collective consciousness is real, individual AI consciousness debates miss the relevant locus; if temporal/developmental processes are constitutive, instant training precludes consciousness regardless of mechanism. The map demands **not more research within current frameworks but examination of the frameworks themselves** — testing whether foundational assumptions (consciousness unity, individual locus, human-centrism, cognition/affect separation) are true or artifacts of disciplinary history. The practical implication is stark: AI deployment proceeds on foundations that are logically contradictory (multiple incompatible intelligence definitions), empirically untested (virtual embodiment, non-Western intelligence integration), and normatively vacant (no flourishing framework), meaning **we cannot coherently evaluate whether AI serves or undermines human life because we haven't resolved what intelligence, consciousness, or human flourishing are**.
